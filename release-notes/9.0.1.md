# 9.0.1

A fix to `Bar`, and two to the tooling around the build. Nothing you call
changes.

## `Bar`'s title no longer sits on top of its buttons

On a narrow bar, `middleContent` and `endContent` could overlap — the title
drawn straight over the button next to it, both readable, neither usable.

It happened whenever the bar was too narrow for everything in it, which on a
component whose whole job is a header means "in a side panel", "in a split
view", or "on a phone".

Nothing to change on your side. If you worked around it by shortening a title or
dropping a slot, you can put them back.

**What changes, in case you were relying on the old behaviour:** when a bar runs
out of room, the **title** is now the thing that gives — it clips — and the start
and end slots keep their size. Previously the outer slots were the ones allowed
to shrink, which is what caused the overlap, because a button inside them does
not shrink with them. A truncated heading is readable; an unreachable button is
not.

## Also

- `deploy.sh` assembled the site from the wrong directory after the demo build
  moved in 8.0.0, so it copied a stale library build instead of the demos. Its
  own verification caught this and refused to publish, which is what that step
  is for — but the site could not be deployed until it was fixed.
- `visual-check.mjs` checked the same stale path before deciding whether a demo
  had been built, so it complained about a correctly-built demo and waved
  through a missing one.

Neither ships in the published packages; both are listed because they are the
reason 9.0.0 never reached the site.
